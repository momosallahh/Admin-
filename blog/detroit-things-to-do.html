15 Best Things to Do in Detroit Michigan 2024

Your complete guide to Motor City's top attractions, from world-class museums to hidden gems that showcase Detroit's incredible comeback story.

Detroit is experiencing a renaissance that's captured the nation's attention. From the historic architecture of downtown to the vibrant energy of Midtown, Motor City offers an incredible mix of culture, history, food, and entertainment that rivals any major American city.

Whether you're a first-time visitor or a proud Detroit homeowner exploring your own backyard, this guide covers the absolute best experiences our city has to offer. 1. Detroit Institute of Arts

The Detroit Institute of Arts (DIA) ranks among America's finest art museums, housing over 65,000 artworks spanning centuries and continents. The crown jewel is Diego Rivera's "Detroit Industry Murals," a masterpiece depicting Detroit's manufacturing heritage. The museum's Beaux-Arts architecture is stunning, and admission is free for residents of Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties thanks to voter-approved millage. Plan at least 3-4 hours to explore the extensive collections. While you're admiring the DIA's magnificent architecture and intricate ceiling details, you might appreciate how proper building maintenance preserves such historic treasures ‚Äì the same care Detroit homeowners should give their roofs.

Location: 5200 Woodward Ave, Detroit | Best Time: Weekday mornings for smaller crowds

2. Belle Isle Park

This 982-acre island park in the Detroit River is a true urban oasis designed by Frederick Law Olmsted, the same landscape architect behind New York's Central Park. Belle Isle offers incredible diversity: the Anna Scripps Whitcomb Conservatory with exotic plants, the Belle Isle Aquarium (America's oldest), the James Scott Memorial Fountain, beaches, hiking trails, and spectacular city skyline views. Rent bikes, enjoy a picnic, or simply relax by the water. The island requires a Michigan Recreation Passport for entry. Visit during sunset for unforgettable views of the Detroit and Windsor skylines reflecting off the river.

Location: Belle Isle, Detroit | Best Time: Spring through fall, sunset hours

3. Eastern Market

Eastern Market is one of America's largest and oldest public markets, operating since 1891. Every Saturday, the market explodes with energy as local farmers, artisans, and food vendors fill the historic sheds with fresh produce, flowers, specialty foods, and handmade goods. The surrounding murals make it one of the world's largest outdoor art galleries. Don't miss Russell Street Deli for amazing sandwiches or Supino Pizzeria for Detroit-style pizza. The market operates year-round with indoor winter hours. Beyond Saturday's main market, visit Tuesday for a smaller farmers market and explore the permanent shops and restaurants that operate all week.

Location: 2934 Russell St, Detroit | Best Time: Saturday mornings (arrive early!)

4. Motown Museum (Hitsville U.S.A.)

Step into the humble house where Berry Gordy Jr. launched the legendary Motown Records label and changed music history forever. This unassuming home at 2648 West Grand Boulevard became the birthplace of hits from The Supremes, Marvin Gaye, Stevie Wonder, and The Temptations. Stand in Studio A where countless classics were recorded, see the original apartment where Gordy lived, and explore memorabilia from Motown's golden era. The passionate tour guides bring Detroit's musical heritage to life. Book tickets online in advance as tours sell out quickly, especially during summer and weekends.

Location: 2648 W Grand Blvd, Detroit | Best Time: Book tours in advance online

5. Detroit RiverWalk

The Detroit RiverWalk stretches over 5.5 miles along the Detroit River waterfront, connecting neighborhoods from Belle Isle to the Ambassador Bridge. This award-winning public space features walking and biking paths, parks, plazas, pavilions, and public art installations. Watch massive freighters pass by, enjoy the international views of Windsor, Canada, or grab a drink at one of the waterfront cafes. Key highlights include the William G. Milliken State Park (Michigan's first urban state park), Outdoor Adventure Center, and numerous fishing piers. The RiverWalk is perfect for jogging, cycling, or leisurely strolls while enjoying spectacular river views.

Location: Detroit River waterfront | Best Time: Spring through fall, especially sunset

6. Fox Theatre

The Fox Theatre stands as one of America's most spectacular entertainment venues. This 1928 movie palace features stunning Asian-inspired architecture with ornate gold leaf, hand-stenciled patterns, and a six-story Grand Lobby that will take your breath away. Today, the Fox hosts Broadway shows, concerts, comedy acts, and special events. Even if you're not seeing a show, book one of their fascinating behind-the-scenes tours to explore this architectural masterpiece. The theater's exterior is equally impressive, especially when illuminated at night. While admiring the Fox's elaborate ceiling work and architectural details, consider how historic buildings require expert craftsmen ‚Äì just like Detroit roofs need experienced professionals.

Location: 2211 Woodward Ave, Detroit | Best Time: Check event calendar for shows and tours

7. Guardian Building

Often called Detroit's "Cathedral of Finance," the Guardian Building is a stunning Art Deco skyscraper completed in 1929. The building's exterior features orange and red brick with intricate details, but the real show is inside. The lobby showcases vibrant Pewabic tile work, vaulted ceilings with colorful geometric patterns, and Native American-inspired motifs that make it one of the most beautiful building interiors in America. The building houses restaurants and shops on the ground level. Take a free self-guided tour during business hours or join organized architecture tours. Don't miss the Pure Detroit shop for authentic Detroit-made products and memorabilia.

Location: 500 Griswold St, Detroit | Best Time: Weekday business hours for lobby access

8. Campus Martius Park

This vibrant public square in the heart of downtown Detroit serves as the city's gathering place year-round. In winter, the park transforms into a winter wonderland with Detroit's official Christmas tree and a popular ice skating rink. Summer brings beach volleyball, outdoor concerts, food trucks, and the park's famous Dancing Fountain synchronized to music. The park hosts numerous free events including movie nights, fitness classes, and cultural festivals. Surrounding the park are excellent restaurants and cafes with outdoor seating. Campus Martius anchors downtown's revitalization and provides a perfect spot to people-watch while enjoying Detroit's energy.

Location: 800 Woodward Ave, Detroit | Best Time: Year-round; ice skating in winter

9. Mexicantown Restaurant District

Mexicantown is Detroit's vibrant Latino cultural heart, centered along Bagley Street and Vernor Highway. This neighborhood offers the best Mexican food in Michigan, with family-owned restaurants serving authentic cuisine passed down through generations. Must-try spots include Xochimilco Restaurant for traditional plates, El Asador Steakhouse for grilled specialties, and Taqueria Mi Pueblo for street-style tacos. Don't miss La Gloria Bakery for pan dulce and Mexican pastries. The neighborhood also features colorful murals, specialty markets, and the annual Cinco de Mayo festival. After a great meal, explore the shops selling traditional crafts, pi√±atas, and imported goods.

Location: Bagley St & Vernor Hwy area, Detroit | Best Time: Lunch or dinner, weekends for full atmosphere

10. Museum of Contemporary Art Detroit (MOCAD)

MOCAD brings cutting-edge contemporary art to Detroit's Midtown neighborhood in a converted auto dealership building that perfectly suits its innovative mission. The museum showcases rotating exhibitions from emerging and established contemporary artists, with a focus on challenging conventional perspectives. Beyond galleries, MOCAD hosts film screenings, concerts, performances, and community events that blur the lines between art forms. The museum's cafe serves coffee and light fare in a creative atmosphere. Admission is relatively affordable, and the museum offers free admission days monthly. MOCAD represents Detroit's thriving arts scene and commitment to cultural innovation.

Location: 4454 Woodward Ave, Detroit | Best Time: Check exhibition calendar online

11. Comerica Park

Home of the Detroit Tigers, Comerica Park offers one of baseball's best gameday experiences. The ballpark features unique attractions like the carousel, Ferris wheel, and tiger sculptures that roar after home runs. The open concourse design provides great views of the Detroit skyline beyond the outfield. Even if you're not a huge baseball fan, attending a Tigers game is a quintessential Detroit summer experience. Arrive early to explore the park's amenities, grab a Coney dog or craft beer, and soak in the atmosphere. The surrounding district features bars and restaurants perfect for pre-game or post-game gatherings.

Location: 2100 Woodward Ave, Detroit | Best Time: April-September baseball season

12. Heidelberg Project

The Heidelberg Project is an outdoor art installation that transformed a blighted Detroit neighborhood into a vibrant outdoor gallery. Artist Tyree Guyton began decorating abandoned houses and vacant lots with found objects, polka dots, and political messages, creating thought-provoking commentary on urban decay, community, and hope. While some original houses have been demolished over the years, the project continues evolving with new installations. This unique attraction sparks conversations about art, urban renewal, and Detroit's resilience. The outdoor nature means it's accessible 24/7, though visiting during daylight hours is recommended for photography and safety.

Location: Heidelberg St, Detroit | Best Time: Daylight hours for best experience

13. Greektown Casino & Dining District

Detroit's Greektown neighborhood offers entertainment, gaming, and authentic Greek cuisine in a lively district centered on Monroe Street. The Greektown Casino provides slot machines, table games, and live entertainment, while the surrounding streets feature traditional Greek restaurants with outdoor patios. Try saganaki (flaming cheese), gyros, and baklava at establishments like Pegasus Taverna or Golden Fleece. The area comes alive at night with neon signs, music, and crowds enjoying the festive atmosphere. Beyond gambling and dining, explore the shops, bakeries, and the nearby Ford Field (home of the Detroit Lions). The district is easily walkable and offers convenient parking garages.

Location: Monroe St between Brush & St. Antoine, Detroit | Best Time: Evening for full atmosphere

14. Corktown Historic District

Corktown is Detroit's oldest neighborhood, established by Irish immigrants in the 1840s. Today, it's one of the city's trendiest areas, mixing historic charm with modern vitality. Michigan Central Station, Ford's massive renovation project, dominates the skyline and symbolizes Detroit's revival. Explore vintage shops, hip cafes, and acclaimed restaurants along Michigan Avenue. Don't miss Slows Bar BQ for award-winning barbecue, Batch Brewing Company for craft beer, or Sugar House for craft cocktails. The neighborhood features beautifully restored Victorian homes that showcase period architecture ‚Äì these historic homes require specialized roofing care to preserve their character while providing modern protection.

Location: Michigan Ave & Trumbull area, Detroit | Best Time: Afternoon through evening

15. Detroit Zoo

Located in nearby Royal Oak, the Detroit Zoo spans 125 acres and houses over 2,400 animals representing 235 species. Highlights include the Arctic Ring of Life (a polar bear habitat with underwater tunnels), the award-winning Penguin Conservation Center, and the Australian Outback Adventure. The zoo prioritizes animal welfare and conservation education. Recent additions include the Polk Penguin Conservation Center, one of the world's largest penguin facilities. The zoo offers year-round experiences, including Wild Lights during winter holidays and summer concert series. Plan for 3-4 hours to explore comfortably. The zoo's commitment to excellence mirrors Elite Roofing's dedication to quality craftsmanship.

Location: 8450 W 10 Mile Rd, Royal Oak | Best Time: Spring and fall for comfortable weather
