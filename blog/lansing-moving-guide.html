üí∞ Cost of Living in Lansing

Lansing offers exceptional affordability with costs 10-12% below the national average. Housing is remarkably affordable, making homeownership accessible even on moderate incomes.

üìä Lansing Cost Breakdown

  • Median Home Price: $150,000
  • Average Rent (1BR): $850/month
  • Average Rent (2BR): $1,050/month
  • Property Taxes: 1.4-1.6% of assessed value
  • Utilities (Winter): $170-$230/month
  • Groceries: 8% below national average
  • Transportation: $55/month (CATA bus pass) or $130-$180 gas/insurance
  • Overall Cost Index: 10-12% below national average

üöó Transportation

Lansing is car-dependent with developing public transit. Traffic is generally light, making commutes quick and stress-free.

üöå Getting Around Lansing

  • Public Transit: CATA bus system (decent coverage, primarily commuter-focused)
  • Major Highways: I-96, I-69, US-127
  • Airport: Capital Region International Airport (LAN) - 15 minutes, Detroit DTW - 90 minutes
  • Walkability: Good in downtown and Old Town, low elsewhere
  • Bike-Friendly: Growing trail network, River Trail popular
  • Parking: Abundant and mostly free, even downtown

Most residents rely on cars for daily life. Traffic is minimal compared to Detroit or Grand Rapids, making car commutes pleasant. Winter weather requires reliable transportation.
