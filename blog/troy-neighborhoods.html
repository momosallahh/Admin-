Troy Neighborhood Guide 2024: Best Places to Live in Troy, MI

Explore Troy's premier Oakland County neighborhoods - from upscale Northfield Hills to family-friendly subdivisions and luxury areas.

Troy represents Oakland County suburban living at its finest. Consistently ranked among America's safest cities, Troy combines excellent schools, diverse housing, thriving business districts, and exceptional quality of life into one highly desirable package.

This affluent Metro Detroit suburb attracts professionals, executives, and families seeking top-tier schools, safe neighborhoods, and convenient access to corporate headquarters. From upscale Northfield Hills estates to family-friendly subdivisions near Somerset Collection, Troy's neighborhoods offer sophistication and stability.

Whether you're relocating for corporate work, prioritizing schools, or seeking upscale suburban living, Elite Roofing serves every Troy neighborhood with premium roofing services. üè∞ Northfield Hills

Character: Troy's most prestigious neighborhood featuring luxury estates on rolling, wooded lots. This is where executives and successful professionals choose to live.

Home Styles: Custom-built luxury estates, contemporary mansions, traditional colonials on 1-3 acre lots. High-end finishes and professional landscaping throughout.

Price Range: $550,000 - $2,500,000+ (Troy's luxury tier)

Schools: Excellent - Troy School District is consistently top-rated in Michigan

Parks & Recreation: Private wooded lots, close to Birmingham shopping/dining, golf courses nearby

Shopping & Dining: Excellent - Somerset Collection 10 minutes, Birmingham 5 minutes, upscale dining throughout area

Walkability: Low - Estate-style properties on large lots, car necessary

Best For: Executives, physicians, successful entrepreneurs, luxury home seekers, those prioritizing privacy

Northfield Hills estates feature premium roofing systems - architectural shingles, metal, slate, or tile. Elite Roofing's master craftsmen provide the specialized expertise these luxury homes require, with experience in complex rooflines and high-end materials.

üè¢ Big Beaver/Crooks Area

Character: Upscale residential neighborhoods surrounding Troy's corporate corridor. Convenient to business districts while maintaining quiet suburban atmosphere.

Home Styles: Well-maintained colonials, contemporary homes, and upscale ranch homes from 1970s-2000s. Mix of original and updated properties.

Price Range: $285,000 - $650,000 (upper-middle to upscale suburban)

Schools: Excellent - Troy School District, multiple highly-rated elementary schools

Parks & Recreation: Troy Community Center, multiple parks, close to shopping and dining

Shopping & Dining: Excellent - Somerset Collection nearby, Big Beaver corridor restaurants, easy freeway access

Walkability: Low to Moderate - Suburban layout, some neighborhoods walkable internally

Best For: Corporate professionals, families prioritizing schools and convenience, commuters

Big Beaver area homes typically feature quality architectural shingles on well-designed rooflines. Elite Roofing provides the premium materials and craftsmanship these valuable homes deserve, with most replacements costing $9,000-$16,000.

Character: Residential neighborhoods near Somerset Collection offering ultimate convenience - walk to luxury shopping while enjoying quiet suburban streets.

Home Styles: Mix of contemporary homes, colonials, and upscale condos/townhomes. Well-maintained properties with professional landscaping.

üèõÔ∏è Troy Historic Village Area

Character: Established family neighborhoods near Troy's historic center. Mix of original Troy residents and young families seeking excellent schools and community.

Home Styles: Ranch homes, colonials, and split-levels from 1950s-1980s. Well-maintained properties with mature trees and landscaping.

Price Range: $235,000 - $485,000 (accessible Troy pricing with excellent schools)

Schools: Excellent - Troy School District, neighborhood elementary schools highly rated

Parks & Recreation: Troy Historic Village, Boulan Park, Stage Nature Center, community events

Shopping & Dining: Good - Commercial corridors on Rochester Road and Livernois, easy access to Somerset

Walkability: Moderate - Safe neighborhood streets, kids walk to school, car needed for shopping

Best For: Families with children, first-time Troy buyers, those seeking community and schools

Historic Village area homes feature traditional rooflines that are cost-effective to maintain. Elite Roofing typically replaces these roofs for $8,000-$13,000 with quality architectural shingles that complement Troy's aesthetic standards.

üè† Troy Home Buying Tips

üè´ All Troy Schools Excellent

Unlike many districts, Troy School District is excellent throughout - no "bad" schools. Focus on home features and location rather than specific school boundaries. All neighborhoods deliver quality education.

üîç Premium Roof Inspection Worth It

Troy homes are valuable investments. Elite Roofing's detailed inspection examines not just shingle condition but ventilation, ice dam potential, and system longevity. On a $500k+ home, comprehensive roof knowledge is essential.

üíº Corporate Relocations Common

Troy attracts corporate relocations, creating steady turnover. Work with experienced agents who understand corporate buyers. Homes near major employers (Big Beaver corridor) may command premiums.

üèõÔ∏è Newer vs. Established Trade-offs

Newer subdivisions offer modern amenities but smaller lots. Established neighborhoods (Historic Village area) provide mature trees and larger lots but may need systems updates including roofing. Budget accordingly.

üí∞ Property Taxes Significant

Troy's excellent services come with higher property taxes. Calculate total ownership costs including taxes (2-3% of value annually). Factor this into budget alongside mortgage and insurance.

üìç Commute Considerations

Troy offers excellent freeway access (I-75, I-696, M-59). Test your commute during rush hour. Some neighborhoods provide easier access to specific freeways - consider your daily route.
