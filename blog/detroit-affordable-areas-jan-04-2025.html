‚úì Buying Tips for Budget Shoppers

How to Find the Best Deals:

1. Look Beyond Median Prices
  • Medians include all conditions - find move-in ready gems
  • Updated homes exist in every price range
  • Don't judge neighborhood by lowest prices
2. Prioritize Location & Condition
  • Better to buy smaller in good area than larger in rough area
  • Check roof, mechanicals, foundation first
  • Cosmetic updates are easy - structural issues aren't
3. Understand What You're Buying
  • Brick Bungalows (1920s-1940s): Durable, character, good bones
  • Ranch Homes (1950s-1960s): Single-story, attached garages
  • Tudors/Colonials (1920s-1930s): More space, architectural details
4. Budget for Maintenance
  • Older homes need upkeep (factor $200-400/month)
  • Roof inspection CRITICAL before purchase
  • Budget for furnace, water heater (10-15 year lifespans)
  • Set aside emergency fund ($3,000-5,000)
5. First-Time Buyer Programs
  • FHA Loans: 3.5% down payment
  • Michigan State Housing Development Authority (MSHDA): Down payment assistance
  • Detroit Home Mortgage: Low down payment options
  • First-Time Buyer Grants: Up to $7,500 assistance

‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags in Affordable Homes:

Critical Issues:
  • Roof Damage: Get professional inspection BEFORE buying
  • Foundation Cracks: Horizontal cracks = major structural issue
  • Electrical: Knob-and-tube wiring needs replacement
  • Plumbing: Lead or galvanized pipes should be replaced
  • Water Damage: Check basement, ceilings for stains
  • HVAC Age: Furnaces last 15-20 years, AC 12-15 years
Neighborhood Research:
  • Visit multiple times (day and evening)
  • Talk to neighbors
  • Check crime statistics (but don't rely solely on)
  • Drive around 5-block radius
  • Research school options
Deal Breakers to Avoid:
  • ‚ùå Completely vacant blocks (no neighbors)
  • ‚ùå Extensive fire damage
  • ‚ùå Sagging roof (major structural)
  • ‚ùå Flooded basement with no drainage
  • ‚ùå No heat source in winter months
