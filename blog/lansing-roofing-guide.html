Common Roofing Problems in Lansing

Based on our experience serving Lansing homeowners for over 15 years, these are the most frequent roofing issues we encounter:

1. Ice Dam Formation (Major Winter Concern)

Lansing's cold winters combined with fluctuating temperatures create ideal conditions for ice dams, which can cause significant water damage.

Solution: Proper attic insulation (R-49 minimum for Michigan), balanced ventilation system, and ice & water shield protection on all eaves. Heat cables for problem areas.

2. Hail Damage from Severe Thunderstorms

Central Michigan experiences frequent severe thunderstorms with hail, particularly May through August.

Solution: Class 4 impact-resistant shingles provide excellent protection and qualify for insurance discounts (typically 10-25% in Lansing area).

3. Wind Damage from Frontal Systems

Lansing's position in the center of the state means it gets hit by strong wind systems from multiple directions.

Solution: High-wind rated shingles with proper installation (6-nail pattern), sealed shingles, and reinforced ridge caps. Essential for exposed locations.

4. Aging Infrastructure in Historic Areas

Old Town and other historic Lansing neighborhoods have homes with older roofing systems that need careful evaluation and updating.

Solution: Comprehensive roof inspections before purchase, proper ventilation retrofits, and modern materials that respect architectural character.

Storm Damage Prevention Tips

Lansing faces varied weather threats throughout the year. Here's how to protect your roof from ice, hail, wind, and severe storms:

üîç Annual Inspections

Schedule professional inspections every spring after winter damage season. Fall inspection prepares roof for coming winter. Catch problems early before they become expensive repairs.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Ice Dam Prevention

Ensure proper attic insulation and ventilation. Install ice & water shield on eaves and valleys. Consider heat cables for chronic problem areas. Keep attic cold to prevent snow melt.

üçÉ Gutter Maintenance

Clean gutters twice yearly (spring and fall minimum). Install quality gutter guards to reduce maintenance. Ensure proper slope for drainage. 6" gutters recommended for heavy rain/snow areas.

üí® Wind Protection

Upgrade to sealed, high-wind rated shingles. Ensure proper fastening (6 nails per shingle). Check and reinforce ridge caps. Trim nearby trees to prevent branch damage during storms.

‚ö° Impact Resistance

Consider Class 4 impact-resistant shingles for hail protection. Qualifies for insurance discounts. Protects against falling branches and storm debris. Excellent long-term investment.

üìû Emergency Preparedness

Have emergency roofing contact ready for severe weather events. Elite Roofing offers 24/7 emergency service for tarping and temporary repairs throughout the Lansing area.
