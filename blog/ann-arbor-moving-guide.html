üí∞ Cost of Living in Ann Arbor

Ann Arbor is Michigan's most expensive city, with costs 25-30% above the state average. However, compared to coastal tech hubs like San Francisco or Boston, it remains remarkably affordable for what you get.

üìä Ann Arbor Cost Breakdown

  • Median Home Price: $390,000
  • Average Rent (1BR): $1,450/month
  • Average Rent (2BR): $1,850/month
  • Property Taxes: 1.5-1.8% of assessed value
  • Utilities (Winter): $150-$210/month
  • Groceries: 10% above national average
  • Transportation: $75/month (bus pass) or $140-$200 gas/insurance
  • Overall Cost Index: 25-30% above national average

The high cost reflects exceptional schools, walkability, strong job market, and quality of life. Most residents find the premium worthwhile, especially families prioritizing education. Salaries are typically higher to compensate, particularly in tech and healthcare.

üéì Schools & Education

Ann Arbor Public Schools is one of Michigan's top-performing districts. Education quality is a primary draw for families relocating here.

üè´ Education Overview

  • Ann Arbor Public Schools: Consistently top-ranked in state and nation

üöó Transportation

Ann Arbor is Michigan's most walkable and bike-friendly city, though most residents still own cars for convenience and winter weather.

üöå Getting Around Ann Arbor

  • Public Transit: TheRide bus system (free in downtown), extensive routes
  • Major Highways: US-23, I-94, M-14
  • Airport: Detroit Metro Airport (DTW) - 30 minutes east
  • Walkability: Excellent downtown and near-campus, good in many neighborhoods
  • Bike-Friendly: Extensive bike lanes, Border-to-Border Trail, bike-share program
  • Parking: Challenging and expensive downtown ($1-$2.50/hour), free in neighborhoods

Many downtown and near-campus residents bike or walk year-round. The University offers free bus service. However, winter weather and suburban spread mean most families still rely on cars.

üè† Home Inspection & Roofing Essentials

Given Ann Arbor's high home prices and prevalence of older homes, thorough inspection is critical. Don't skip roof evaluation - it's expensive to replace.

üîç What to Inspect Before Buying

  • Roof Age: Critical given Ann Arbor's premium home prices
  • Historic Home Issues: Many homes have complex, multi-level roofs prone to problems
  • Ice Dam Evidence: Check attics carefully for staining
  • Flashing Quality: Older homes often have deteriorated flashing
  • Ventilation: Poor ventilation accelerates roof deterioration
  • Tree Damage: Tree Town lives up to its name - check for branch damage
