15 Best Things to Do in Livonia Michigan 2024

Your complete guide to Wayne County's largest suburb, from historic sites to beautiful parks and family-friendly attractions.

Livonia offers classic suburban living with excellent schools, beautiful parks, diverse shopping and dining, and a strong sense of community. As one of Metro Detroit's largest suburbs, Livonia provides residents and visitors with quality experiences from historic preservation to modern recreation, all while maintaining the neighborhood feel that makes it one of Michigan's most desirable places to call home.

Whether you're exploring Livonia for the first time or you're a local homeowner enjoying your community, this guide highlights the best experiences our city offers. 1. Greenmead Historical Park

Greenmead Historical Park preserves Livonia's agricultural heritage with 95 acres featuring historic buildings, walking trails, and educational programs. The park's centerpiece is the Simmons House, a beautiful Victorian farmhouse surrounded by period outbuildings including a one-room schoolhouse, church, general store, and barns. The park hosts special events throughout the year including Civil War reenactments, vintage baseball games, and the popular Christmas Walk. Educational programs serve school groups teaching local history and pioneer life. The grounds include nature trails through woods and meadows. The park demonstrates Livonia's commitment to preserving rural heritage amid suburban development. The historic structures showcase 19th-century construction methods requiring specialized preservation techniques to maintain their character for future generations.

Location: 38125 W 8 Mile Rd, Livonia | Best Time: Summer for full programming; special events year-round

2. Rotary Park

Rotary Park serves as Livonia's premier park facility with extensive recreational amenities serving families and individuals year-round. The park features baseball fields, soccer fields, tennis courts, basketball courts, and playground equipment. The modern recreation center includes fitness facilities, indoor walking track, and program spaces. Outdoor trails wind through the park connecting activity areas. The park hosts community events, youth sports leagues, and seasonal celebrations. Well-maintained facilities and attractive landscaping make it inviting for all ages. The park includes picnic areas with shelters available for reservation. Summer brings concerts and special events. The park represents Livonia's investment in quality recreation facilities that enhance community life and provide gathering spaces for residents.

Location: 15000 Hubbard Rd, Livonia | Best Time: Spring through fall for outdoor activities

3. Laurel Park Place

Laurel Park Place offers upscale shopping and dining in an attractive indoor mall environment. The mall features quality retailers including Von Maur, J.Crew, and specialty boutiques alongside popular brands. The dining options range from quick casual to full-service restaurants including The Cheesecake Factory, Panera, and other favorites. The mall's elegant interior design creates pleasant shopping experiences with comfortable seating areas and attractive landscaping. The mall serves as a community gathering place beyond just shopping. Regular hours and climate-controlled environment provide year-round convenience. The retail mix balances national chains with unique local boutiques. Events and seasonal promotions add variety throughout the year. The mall demonstrates successful suburban retail serving diverse customer needs.

Location: 37700 W 6 Mile Rd, Livonia | Best Time: Weekdays for less crowded shopping

4. Livonia Recreation Center

The Livonia Recreation Center provides comprehensive fitness and recreation facilities serving residents with modern amenities and diverse programs. The center features a fitness area with cardio and strength equipment, indoor walking track, gymnasium for basketball and volleyball, and multipurpose rooms for classes and activities. Programs include fitness classes, youth sports, senior activities, and special events. The facility offers memberships and day passes making it accessible to all. The recreation center represents Livonia's commitment to health and wellness through quality public facilities. Professional staff provide instruction and support. The center serves as a community hub where neighbors connect through shared activities. The modern facility demonstrates investment in recreational infrastructure supporting active lifestyles.

Location: 15100 Hubbard Rd, Livonia | Best Time: Year-round; check class schedules

5. Livonia Public Library System

Livonia Public Library operates multiple branches providing excellent library services throughout the community. The libraries offer extensive collections of books, magazines, DVDs, audiobooks, and digital resources. Comfortable reading areas, study spaces, and computer labs serve diverse needs. Programs for all ages include storytimes for children, book clubs, technology classes, author visits, and cultural events. The libraries provide free WiFi, printing services, and meeting rooms. The Civic Center Library and other branches serve neighborhood needs with convenient access. Special collections and displays rotate regularly. The library system demonstrates Livonia's commitment to literacy, education, and lifelong learning. The modern facilities create welcoming community spaces supporting information access and cultural enrichment.

Location: Multiple branches throughout Livonia | Best Time: Year-round during operating hours

6. Hines Park & Hines Drive Trail

Hines Park runs through Livonia as part of a larger Wayne County park system following the Middle Rouge River. The park provides beautiful natural areas within suburban surroundings with trails, picnic areas, and scenic drives. The paved Hines Drive Trail serves cyclists, runners, and walkers with miles of paths through wooded areas along the river. The park includes playgrounds, shelters available for reservation, and open spaces for activities. Wildlife viewing opportunities include waterfowl and woodland birds. The park connects to regional trail systems extending recreational opportunities. Fall brings spectacular color displays. The park demonstrates the value of protecting river corridors for both environmental and recreational purposes. Summer weekends bring families enjoying picnics and outdoor activities.

Location: Hines Dr through Livonia | Best Time: Spring through fall; early mornings or evenings

7. Newburgh Lake

Newburgh Lake offers waterfront recreation within Livonia with fishing, kayaking, and scenic trails around the lake. The lake supports diverse fish populations making it popular with anglers targeting bass, pike, and panfish. Kayak and canoe launches provide water access for paddlers exploring the shoreline. Walking trails around the lake offer peaceful exercise with water views. The park includes picnic areas and benches for relaxing. Wildlife viewing opportunities include waterfowl, herons, and turtles. The lake provides habitat for aquatic species while offering recreation. Spring and fall migrations bring interesting bird species. The lake represents important green space and water resources within suburban development. Summer sunsets over the water attract photographers and nature lovers.

Location: Hines Park area, Livonia | Best Time: Spring through fall for water activities

The market creates community gathering spaces where residents connect with local food producers and neighbors. 13. Civic Center Park

Civic Center Park anchors Livonia's municipal complex with city offices, library, and community facilities surrounding attractive parkland. The park features walking paths, open lawns, and landscaped areas creating peaceful public spaces in the city center. The area hosts community events, outdoor concerts, and seasonal celebrations. The farmers market operates from the nearby Wilson Barn. The civic center design creates a community hub where residents access services, attend events, and enjoy public spaces. Monuments and public art add interest. The park demonstrates thoughtful civic planning integrating government functions with community gathering spaces. The well-maintained grounds reflect municipal pride. The area serves as Livonia's ceremonial center for official events and community celebrations.

Location: 33000 Civic Center Dr, Livonia | Best Time: Year-round; summer for events

15. Biking & Walking Trails

Livonia offers paved trails and bike paths throughout the city providing safe routes for cycling, walking, and jogging. The trail system connects parks, schools, and neighborhoods creating non-motorized transportation options and recreational routes. Popular trails include the Hines Drive Trail and paths through park systems. The expanding network demonstrates Livonia's commitment to alternative transportation and active lifestyles. Well-maintained surfaces and clear signage make trails accessible to all ages and abilities. Families use trails for bike rides and walks together. Fitness enthusiasts appreciate dedicated spaces for exercise. The trails provide health benefits while supporting environmental goals by encouraging non-automobile transportation. The system shows how mature suburbs can adapt infrastructure to support walking and cycling.

Location: Trail access points throughout Livonia | Best Time: Spring through fall; early mornings or evenings
