1. Spencer

Cuisine: Contemporary American | Price: $$$$ | Best For: Special Occasions, Tasting Menus

Spencer represents Ann Arbor's fine dining pinnacle, where chef Abby Kumin creates sophisticated tasting menus that showcase Michigan ingredients with impeccable technique and creative vision. The intimate 48-seat restaurant features an open kitchen where diners can watch the culinary team orchestrate complex dishes with precision. The ever-changing menu might include creative preparations of local fish, foraged ingredients, house-made pastas, and unexpected vegetable presentations that elevate produce to star status. The beverage program features expertly curated wine pairings alongside creative non-alcoholic options. The minimalist, modern space allows the food to be the focus while providing comfortable, elegant surroundings. Service is warm and knowledgeable, making fine dining approachable rather than intimidating. Reservations essential, often booking weeks in advance for weekend evenings. Dress code is smart casual. This is destination dining worthy of celebrating life's special moments.

Location: 207 E Washington St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Experience: Multi-course tasting menu

2. Aventura

Cuisine: Spanish Tapas | Price: $$-$$$ | Best For: Date Night, Groups, Wine Lovers

Aventura brings authentic Spanish cuisine and culture to Ann Arbor with tapas, paella, and an impressive Spanish wine list. The menu features traditional preparations alongside creative seasonal offerings ‚Äì from classic patatas bravas and gambas al ajillo to innovative small plates that showcase local ingredients within a Spanish framework. The paella, available in several varieties, is a showstopper requiring advance ordering but worth planning dinner around. The wine program focuses exclusively on Spanish selections, with knowledgeable servers guiding pairings. The cozy, intimate space creates romantic atmosphere perfect for date nights, while the tapas format works beautifully for groups wanting to share multiple dishes. Weekend flamenco performances add authentic cultural elements. The restaurant demonstrates how Ann Arbor embraces international cuisine while supporting local farmers. Located near the University of Michigan campus, it attracts diverse crowds from students celebrating to professors entertaining visitors.

Location: 216 E Washington St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Specialty: Paella and Spanish wines

3. Zingerman's Delicatessen

Cuisine: Deli/Jewish | Price: $$ | Best For: Lunch, Ann Arbor Institution, Sandwiches

Zingerman's Deli has achieved legendary status since opening in 1982, earning national recognition for traditional Jewish deli fare made with obsessive attention to quality. The towering sandwiches feature house-baked breads, premium meats, imported cheeses, and creative combinations that balance tradition with innovation. The Reuben consistently ranks among America's best, while creative options like the "Z-man" showcase what's possible when quality ingredients meet culinary creativity. Beyond sandwiches, the deli offers amazing baked goods, specialty foods, imported products, and made-to-order salads. The counter service can mean lines during peak hours, but efficient staff keeps things moving. The original location offers the full deli experience, while Zingerman's has expanded into a community of businesses including a bakery, creamery, coffee roaster, and training center. This is essential Ann Arbor eating ‚Äì a local institution that lives up to its national reputation. Plan to spend time browsing the specialty food selection.

Location: 422 Detroit St, Ann Arbor (Kerrytown) | Must-Order: The Reuben sandwich

4. Tomukun Noodle Bar

Cuisine: Korean Noodles | Price: $ | Best For: Lunch, Casual Dining, Students

Tomukun Noodle Bar serves exceptional Korean noodle dishes at remarkably affordable prices, creating lines out the door during lunch hours. The focused menu features various Korean noodle preparations ‚Äì spicy beef soup, cold noodles, and specialty bowls that showcase complex, layered flavors. The broths are rich and authentic, the noodles perfectly cooked, and the accompaniments generous. The spice levels can be adjusted, though even "mild" packs serious heat. The compact counter-service space seats maybe 20 people, creating a cozy, communal atmosphere. Turnover is quick despite the popularity. Cash only, so plan accordingly. The rock-bottom prices (most bowls under $10) and big portions make it a favorite among students, but the quality attracts everyone from professors to downtown workers. Located near campus, it represents Ann Arbor's incredible diversity of affordable, authentic international cuisine. After warming up with amazing noodles, remember Elite Roofing keeps Ann Arbor homes comfortable year-round.

Location: 505 E Liberty St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Note: Cash only, expect lines

5. Frita Batidos

Cuisine: Cuban Street Food | Price: $ | Best For: Casual Dining, Groups, Late Night

Frita Batidos brings Cuban street food culture to Ann Arbor with creative fritas (Cuban-style burgers), tropical batidos (milkshakes), and vibrant atmosphere. Chef Eve Aronoff's menu features the signature fritas ‚Äì seasoned ground meat patties on Cuban bread with crispy shoestring potatoes piled on top ‚Äì alongside creative variations using chorizo, fish, or vegetarian options. The batidos come in tropical flavors like mango, guava, and passionfruit, or creative boozy versions for adults. Sides include yucca fries, plantain chips, and Cuban black beans. The colorful, casual space features communal tables and energetic vibes. Counter service keeps things moving efficiently. Moderate prices and generous portions make it easy to bring groups. The restaurant captures Cuban street food's essence ‚Äì bold flavors, fun atmosphere, and accessible prices. Located in the heart of downtown, it attracts late-night crowds looking for satisfying food after bars close. The innovative approach to Cuban cuisine has earned national attention and loyal local following.

Location: 117 W Washington St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Must-Try: Fritas and tropical batidos

6. The Chop House

Cuisine: Classic Steakhouse | Price: $$$-$$$$ | Best For: Business Dinners, Celebrations

The Chop House delivers traditional steakhouse excellence in Ann Arbor with USDA Prime aged beef, fresh seafood, and professional service in upscale surroundings. The menu features classic cuts ‚Äì filet mignon, ribeye, New York strip ‚Äì aged and prepared to perfection. Signature preparations like the filet Oscar or peppercorn-crusted ribeye showcase refined technique. Fresh seafood options include lobster tail, king crab legs, and daily fish selections. Traditional sides like creamed spinach, loaded baked potatoes, and asparagus with hollandaise round out the offerings. The extensive wine list emphasizes California cabernets and other beef-friendly selections. The elegant dining room suits business dinners, anniversary celebrations, and special occasions when you want classic American fine dining. Service is attentive and professional without being stuffy. Valet parking adds convenience. Located downtown near the University of Michigan campus, it serves as a go-to spot for visiting parents, business entertainment, and celebrations.

Location: 322 S Main St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Best For: Traditional steakhouse experience

7. Mani Osteria & Bar

Cuisine: Italian | Price: $$-$$$ | Best For: Date Night, Italian Food Lovers

Mani Osteria brings authentic Italian cuisine to Ann Arbor with house-made pastas, wood-fired pizzas, and seasonal preparations that honor Italian traditions while showcasing local ingredients. The pasta program shines with classics like cacio e pepe and carbonara alongside creative seasonal offerings. The wood-fired pizzas feature perfectly charred crusts and quality toppings imported from Italy where appropriate. Antipasti selections provide excellent starters for sharing. The wine list emphasizes Italian selections across all regions and price points. The warm, rustic-modern space creates inviting atmosphere suitable for romantic dinners or gathering with friends. Weekend brunch adds Italian twists to morning classics. The restaurant occupies a beautifully restored space in downtown Ann Arbor, demonstrating how historic buildings can be adapted for modern use while preserving their character ‚Äì much like Elite Roofing's approach to protecting Ann Arbor's diverse housing stock. Service is knowledgeable and friendly, making Italian wine and food approachable rather than intimidating.

Location: 341 E Liberty St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Specialty: House-made pasta and wood-fired pizza

8. Pacific Rim by Kana

Cuisine: Asian Fusion & Sushi | Price: $$ | Best For: Sushi, Casual Asian Dining

Pacific Rim serves creative Asian fusion cuisine and excellent sushi in a modern, casual environment. The menu spans pan-Asian influences ‚Äì Japanese sushi and ramen, Thai curries, Vietnamese pho, and Korean bulgogi ‚Äì all executed with care and authentic flavors. The sushi program features both traditional nigiri and creative specialty rolls that balance innovation with technique. The ramen offerings include rich, complex broths with perfectly cooked noodles. Vegetarian and vegan options are plentiful and creative rather than afterthoughts. The modern space features clean lines and comfortable seating. Lunch specials offer exceptional value. The diverse menu means groups with different preferences can all find something appealing. Bubble tea and Asian-inspired cocktails complement the food. Service is efficient and friendly. Located near campus, it attracts students, faculty, and locals looking for quality Asian cuisine at reasonable prices. The consistency and breadth of menu make it a reliable choice for diverse groups.

Location: 114 W Washington St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Variety: Pan-Asian menu with sushi focus

9. Arbor Brewing Company

Cuisine: Brewpub American | Price: $$ | Best For: Beer Lovers, Casual Dining

Arbor Brewing Company has anchored Ann Arbor's craft beer scene since 1995, serving house-brewed beers alongside elevated pub fare in a welcoming atmosphere. The beer selection rotates seasonally but typically includes their flagship Sacred Cow IPA, creative seasonal offerings, and specialty brews. The food menu goes beyond typical bar food with creative burgers, fresh salads, vegetarian options, and daily specials that show culinary ambition. The fish and chips, made with beer-battered local fish, is a perennial favorite. The outdoor courtyard is perfect during warmer months. The brewery's commitment to sustainability includes solar panels and local sourcing. The space works equally well for casual dinners, meeting friends for drinks, or weekend brunch featuring creative morning cocktails. A second downtown location (Corner Brewery) offers slightly different atmosphere with more focus on experimental beers. Arbor Brewing represents Ann Arbor's progressive, environmentally conscious character while serving as a community gathering spot.

Location: 114 E Washington St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Must-Try: Sacred Cow IPA and burgers

10. Ma Lou's Fried Chicken

Cuisine: Comfort Food | Price: $ | Best For: Casual Dining, Comfort Food Cravings

Ma Lou's Fried Chicken serves exceptional fried chicken and Southern-inspired comfort food in a casual, no-frills environment. The chicken is brined, seasoned, and fried to crispy-skinned, juicy perfection ‚Äì some of Michigan's best fried chicken without question. Sides include creamy mac and cheese, collard greens, cornbread, and other Southern classics executed with care. The portions are generous and prices remarkably affordable. The casual counter-service format and simple space put the focus entirely on the food. Weekend brunch adds chicken and waffles and other morning options. The chicken sandwich has developed cult following among students and locals. Limited seating means takeout is popular, especially during peak hours. Despite the simple setting, the quality rivals much pricier restaurants. The commitment to doing one thing exceptionally well has created devoted following. Cash and cards accepted. This is comfort food at its finest ‚Äì unpretentious, delicious, and satisfying.

Location: 1039 Broadway St, Ann Arbor (North Campus area) | Signature: Perfectly fried chicken

11. Taste Kitchen

Cuisine: New American | Price: $$-$$$ | Best For: Brunch, Seasonal Dining

Taste Kitchen serves creative New American cuisine with emphasis on seasonal, locally sourced ingredients in a warm, inviting space. The menu changes regularly based on what's available from Michigan farms and producers. Expect creative preparations of familiar ingredients ‚Äì vegetables become stars, local fish is treated with reverence, and Michigan-raised meats are showcased simply to highlight quality. Weekend brunch is particularly popular, featuring creative takes on breakfast classics alongside lunch options. The beverage program includes craft cocktails, Michigan wines, and local beers. The farm-to-table philosophy means the menu evolves with seasons, keeping regular visitors discovering new dishes. The casual but polished atmosphere works for everything from business lunches to date nights to family dinners. Service is friendly and knowledgeable about sourcing and preparation. The commitment to supporting local producers aligns with Ann Arbor's values while ensuring the freshest possible ingredients. Reservations recommended for weekend brunch and dinner service.

Location: 310 S Main St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Philosophy: Seasonal and local sourcing

12. Sava's

Cuisine: Upscale American | Price: $$-$$$ | Best For: Brunch, Happy Hour, Date Night

Sava's offers upscale American cuisine in a sophisticated yet welcoming environment that's become an Ann Arbor favorite for everything from power lunches to romantic dinners. The menu balances creative seasonal dishes with reliable favorites ‚Äì fresh salads, innovative sandwiches, well-executed entrees featuring local ingredients when possible. Weekend brunch is legendary, with creative benedicts, fluffy pancakes, and morning cocktails that rival dinner service for popularity. The bar program features craft cocktails made with house-made ingredients and premium spirits. The wine list emphasizes quality selections across price points. Happy hour offers excellent value with discounted appetizers and drinks. The sophisticated interior features warm colors, comfortable seating, and touches of elegance without pretension. Service is professional and attentive. Multiple locations serve different Ann Arbor neighborhoods, each maintaining consistent quality while adapting slightly to their area. The restaurant demonstrates how upscale dining can remain approachable and welcoming to diverse crowds.

Location: 216 S State St, Ann Arbor (Downtown) | Best For: Brunch and happy hour

Main Street and the surrounding downtown area feature the highest concentration of restaurants ‚Äì from Spencer's fine dining to Frita Batidos' casual Cuban fare. Excellent walkability makes restaurant hopping easy. Perfect for pre-theater dining or exploring multiple spots in one evening.

üç∫ Kerrytown

This historic district houses Zingerman's Deli plus numerous other excellent restaurants and the Ann Arbor Farmers Market. Great for combining lunch with market shopping on Saturdays. More laid-back vibe than downtown with easier parking.

üéì Campus Area

The streets surrounding University of Michigan campus offer diverse, affordable international cuisine. Perfect for casual dining and discovering ethnic restaurants serving students from around the world. South University and Liberty Street corridors offer the most options.
