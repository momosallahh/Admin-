üí∞ Cost of Living in Troy

Troy offers an upscale suburban lifestyle with a cost of living slightly above the national average, justified by excellent schools, safety, and amenities. For families prioritizing education and quality of life, Troy delivers exceptional value.

üìä Troy Cost Breakdown

  • Median Home Price: $310,000 (range $200k-$800k+)
  • Average Rent (1BR): $1,350/month
  • Average Rent (2BR): $1,650/month
  • Property Taxes: $3,500-$8,000/year (effective rate ~2.5% of assessed value)
  • Utilities (Winter): $200-$280/month (heating costs peak December-February)
  • Groceries: Slightly above national average (premium grocery options available)
  • Transportation: Car-dependent; gas/insurance $200-$300/month
  • Overall Cost Index: 8-12% above national average

üöó Transportation

Troy is car-dependent but offers excellent highway access and manageable traffic. The city's central location provides easy access throughout metro Detroit.

üöå Getting Around Troy

  • Public Transit: SMART bus system with limited routes; most residents drive
  • Major Highways: I-75 (north-south), I-696 (east-west), Big Beaver Road (M-1)
  • Airport: Detroit Metro Airport (DTW) - 35 minutes; Oakland County Airport (nearby)
  • Walkability: Moderate in Somerset area; low in residential neighborhoods
  • Bike-Friendly: Improving with trails; not bike-commuter friendly overall
  • Parking: Abundant and free at most locations

Commute Access: I-75 provides quick access to Detroit (20 minutes), Pontiac (15 minutes), and Ann Arbor (45 minutes). I-696 connects to Southfield and western suburbs. Traffic is generally manageable outside peak hours (7-9am, 4-6pm).

Winter Driving: Troy's roads are well-maintained in winter, but snow tires or all-weather tires are recommended. Budget for a reliable vehicle capable of handling Michigan winters.

üè† Home Inspection & Roofing Essentials

Troy homes are generally well-maintained, but thorough inspection is essential - especially for roofs. Michigan winters and summer storms take their toll even on quality construction.

üîç What to Inspect Before Buying

  • Roof Age & Condition: Most Troy homes have 15-30 year asphalt roofs; verify remaining lifespan
  • Ice Dam History: Check attic for water stains indicating past ice dam damage
  • Gutters & Downspouts: Proper drainage crucial for basement protection
  • Attic Ventilation: Essential for preventing ice dams and extending roof life
  • Insulation: Critical for energy efficiency in Michigan climate
  • Flashing & Valleys: Common failure points, especially on older roofs
  • Basement: Check for water intrusion, especially in spring
