Common Roofing Problems in Detroit

Based on our experience serving Detroit homeowners for over 15 years, these are the most frequent roofing issues we encounter:

1. Ice Dams (Most Common Winter Issue)

Ice dams form when heat escapes through your roof, melting snow that refreezes at the eaves. This creates a dam that backs water under your shingles.

Solution: Proper attic insulation and ventilation are key. We also recommend heated cables for problem areas.

2. Wind Damage from Lake Effect Systems

Detroit's proximity to Lake Erie means we get powerful wind systems, especially in fall and winter.

Solution: High-wind rated shingles and proper installation techniques with extra fasteners.

3. Granule Loss from Freeze-Thaw Cycles

The constant freezing and thawing damages shingles, causing granule loss and reducing their lifespan.

Solution: Premium architectural shingles designed for northern climates last 25-30 years vs. 15-20 for standard shingles.

4. Poor Ventilation in Historic Homes

Many of Detroit's beautiful historic homes weren't designed with modern ventilation in mind.

Solution: Adding ridge vents and soffit vents without compromising architectural integrity.
