Flint's Extreme Affordability

Flint, Michigan offers one of America's lowest costs of living, driven primarily by extremely affordable housing ($30K-60K median homes). While incomes are also lower than national average, the reduced living costs create opportunities for financial freedom unavailable in most cities. üè† Hidden Costs of Flint Homeownership

What First-Time Flint Buyers Often Miss:

1. Roof Replacement ($6K-12K)

Many cheap Flint homes have old roofs. Michigan winters destroy bad roofs. This is often the BIGGEST unexpected expense for new Flint homeowners.

2. Furnace Replacement ($3K-6K)

Older homes, older furnaces. In Flint, heat is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Dead furnace in January = emergency.

3. Plumbing Issues ($1K-10K)

Lead pipes (water crisis concern), galvanized pipes, old plumbing common in Flint's older housing stock.

4. Electrical Updates ($2K-8K)

Knob-and-tube wiring, outdated panels, insufficient amperage in many older Flint homes.

5. Foundation Repairs ($5K-20K+)

Settlement issues, water infiltration in basements, cracking common in older Michigan homes.

Protecting Your Investment:

CRITICAL: Get professional home inspection BEFORE buying. Know what you're getting into. Budget for repairs.
