15 Best Things to Do in Lansing Michigan 2024

Your complete guide to Michigan's capital city, from historic government buildings to vibrant neighborhoods, museums, and outdoor recreation.

Lansing serves as Michigan's capital city, blending governmental importance with a thriving cultural scene, excellent museums, and diverse dining options. The Grand River flows through the heart of the city, connecting parks and trails, while neighborhoods from downtown to Old Town offer unique character and attractions that showcase the region's history and innovation.

Whether you're visiting on government business or you're a Lansing homeowner exploring your community, this guide highlights the best experiences our capital offers. 1. Michigan State Capitol

The Michigan State Capitol is a stunning example of Victorian-era architecture, completed in 1879 and designated a National Historic Landmark. Free guided tours explore the building's ornate cast iron dome, hand-painted ceiling details, and historical legislative chambers. The restored building showcases period craftsmanship with intricate tile work, carved wood details, and period furnishings. Visitors can observe the legislature in session when in operation. The capitol grounds feature monuments and landscaped gardens. Tours depart regularly from the tour desk on the ground floor. The building's dome restoration and preservation demonstrate how historic structures require specialized expertise ‚Äì just as Lansing's historic homes need expert roofing care to maintain their architectural integrity.

Location: 100 N Capitol Ave, Lansing | Best Time: Weekdays for tours; check schedule online

2. Potter Park Zoo

Potter Park Zoo has served the Lansing community since 1920, evolving into a modern conservation-focused facility caring for over 500 animals representing 160 species. Highlights include the Critically Endangered Animals exhibit, the Snow Leopard habitat, and the Red Panda Forest. The zoo participates in species survival programs for endangered animals. Recent additions include updated habitats prioritizing animal welfare and visitor education. The zoo offers special experiences including behind-the-scenes tours, animal encounters, and seasonal events like Zoo Lights during winter holidays. The adjacent Potter Park features playgrounds, disc golf, and trails along the Red Cedar River. Plan 2-3 hours to explore the zoo's compact but well-designed grounds.

Location: 1301 S Pennsylvania Ave, Lansing | Best Time: Spring and fall for comfortable weather

3. Impression 5 Science Center

Impression 5 Science Center engages children and families with over 200 hands-on exhibits exploring science, technology, engineering, and math. Located along the Grand River, the museum features exhibits on bubbles, light, chemistry, engineering challenges, and more. The outdoor Science Park extends learning into nature with water play, construction areas, and physics demonstrations. Special programs include camps, workshops, and visiting exhibitions. The museum emphasizes experiential learning where kids discover concepts through play and experimentation. Birthday parties and field trips are popular. The facility's interactive approach makes science accessible and fun for all ages. Plan at least 2-3 hours to fully explore the exhibits and outdoor areas during warm months.

Location: 200 Museum Dr, Lansing | Best Time: Weekdays for smaller crowds; summer for outdoor exhibits

4. Michigan State University Campus

Michigan State University's beautiful campus in neighboring East Lansing spans over 5,200 acres, making it one of America's largest single-campus universities. The campus features stunning architecture mixing historic ivy-covered buildings with modern facilities. Don't miss Beaumont Tower, the iconic campus landmark, or the serene Japanese Tea House and Gardens. The campus offers free museums including the MSU Museum and Broad Art Museum. Walk along the Red Cedar River or explore the botanical gardens and horticulture displays. MSU's campus is open to visitors year-round with free parking on weekends and designated visitor lots during weekdays. The diverse campus architecture showcases building styles spanning over 150 years.

Location: East Lansing (adjacent to Lansing) | Best Time: Fall for football season; spring for blooms

5. Michigan History Museum

The Michigan History Museum tells the comprehensive story of Michigan from prehistoric times through modern day. Three floors of exhibits include a recreated copper mine, 1957 Detroit street scene, and exhibits on Michigan's automotive heritage, lumber industry, and Native American cultures. Interactive displays engage visitors of all ages. The museum's location across from the Capitol makes it easy to visit both attractions in one trip. Special exhibitions bring focused topics to deeper exploration. The museum shop offers Michigan-themed books, gifts, and historical reproductions. Free admission makes this an accessible educational resource. The museum building itself demonstrates mid-century architecture adapted for museum purposes while maintaining period character.

Location: 702 W Kalamazoo St, Lansing | Best Time: Weekdays for quieter visits

6. Lansing River Trail

The Lansing River Trail extends over 20 miles along the Grand River, connecting parks, neighborhoods, and attractions throughout the Greater Lansing area. The paved multi-use trail serves cyclists, runners, walkers, and inline skaters. The trail passes through downtown, Old Town, and natural areas, offering diverse scenery from urban to wooded riverbanks. Rest areas, overlooks, and park connections provide stopping points. The trail links to Potter Park, Adado Riverfront Park, and other green spaces. It's accessible year-round with some sections maintained for winter use. The trail system demonstrates Lansing's commitment to outdoor recreation and non-motorized transportation. Rent bikes from local shops or bring your own for exploring this extensive trail network.

Location: Multiple access points throughout Lansing | Best Time: Spring through fall; early mornings or evenings

7. Old Town Lansing

Old Town is Lansing's historic commercial district, revitalized into a vibrant neighborhood featuring galleries, boutiques, restaurants, and live music venues. The district's Victorian-era buildings house independent businesses selling art, antiques, vintage clothing, and handmade goods. Restaurants range from casual cafes to upscale dining, with many featuring locally-sourced ingredients. The Old Town General Store offers unique gifts and local products. Friday nights bring the bustling Old Town Farmers Market. Live music venues host local and touring musicians. The district hosts festivals and events year-round. Walking the brick sidewalks past restored buildings shows how historic preservation creates thriving modern neighborhoods while honoring architectural heritage that requires ongoing maintenance and care.

Location: Turner St & Grand River Ave area | Best Time: Friday evenings for farmers market; weekends

8. Wharton Center for Performing Arts

Wharton Center on the MSU campus ranks among the Midwest's premier performing arts venues, hosting Broadway tours, concerts, dance companies, and special events. The center features two theaters with excellent acoustics and sightlines. Recent Broadway shows, symphony performances, and family entertainment provide diverse programming year-round. The center's lobby and public spaces showcase modern design. Pre-show dining options exist on campus and in nearby East Lansing. Free parking is available for evening performances. The professional productions and world-class artists make Wharton Center a cultural anchor for the Greater Lansing region. Season subscriptions and individual tickets available for all events. Check the performance calendar for upcoming shows spanning all artistic genres.

Location: 750 E Shaw Ln, East Lansing | Best Time: Check performance schedule year-round

9. MSU Broad Art Museum

The Eli and Edythe Broad Art Museum features striking contemporary architecture by Zaha Hadid housing a collection focused on contemporary and modern art. The building itself is an architectural landmark with angular forms and creative use of light. Free admission provides access to rotating exhibitions showcasing established and emerging contemporary artists. The museum emphasizes challenging perspectives and innovative artistic practices. Educational programs include gallery talks, workshops, and community events. The museum cafe serves coffee and light fare in a creative atmosphere. The museum represents MSU's commitment to contemporary culture and public engagement with living artists. The building's distinctive roof design demonstrates how architectural innovation requires specialized engineering and construction expertise.

Location: 547 E Circle Dr, East Lansing | Best Time: Check exhibition calendar; free admission

10. Spartan Stadium & MSU Athletics

Spartan Stadium hosts Michigan State University football games, creating electric autumn Saturdays with over 75,000 fans cheering "Go Green, Go White!" The gameday atmosphere transforms East Lansing with tailgates, marching band performances, and Spartan pride throughout campus. Beyond football, MSU offers excellent basketball at Breslin Center, hockey at Munn Ice Arena, and other sports throughout the year. The athletic facilities showcase modern sports architecture. Attending a Spartan sporting event provides quintessential college experience. Stadium tours offer behind-the-scenes access during off-season. The surrounding campus fills with fans before and after games. Rivalry games against Michigan and other Big Ten schools sell out quickly, so book tickets early for best selection.

Location: 325 W Shaw Ln, East Lansing | Best Time: Fall Saturdays for football

11. Fenner Nature Center

Fenner Nature Center offers 134 acres of forests, wetlands, prairies, and trails providing peaceful nature experiences within Lansing city limits. Five miles of trails wind through diverse habitats perfect for hiking, birdwatching, and nature photography. The visitor center features exhibits on local ecosystems and wildlife. Educational programs include guided nature walks, workshops, and children's programs. The center's maple syrup demonstration in early spring is a beloved annual tradition. Cross-country ski and snowshoe trails operate during winter months. The center emphasizes environmental education and conservation. Admission is free, making it an accessible outdoor resource. The preserve demonstrates the importance of protecting natural areas within urban environments for community health and wildlife habitat.

Location: 2020 E Mount Hope Ave, Lansing | Best Time: Year-round; spring for wildflowers, fall for colors

12. Eastwood Towne Center

Eastwood Towne Center provides outdoor lifestyle shopping and dining in an attractive open-air setting. The center features major retailers, specialty stores, restaurants, and entertainment options including a movie theater. The walkable layout with landscaped pedestrian paths creates pleasant shopping experiences. Restaurants range from quick casual to full-service dining with outdoor patios. The center hosts seasonal events and provides ample free parking. It's become a popular gathering place for Lansing area residents. Beyond shopping, the center offers services from banking to healthcare. The outdoor design allows for comfortable browsing during pleasant weather. Evening brings dinner and movie combinations. The center represents modern retail development combining convenience with appealing public spaces.

Location: 2500 Showtime Dr, Lansing | Best Time: Afternoons and evenings; weekends

13. Lansing Lugnuts Baseball

The Lansing Lugnuts play Class A baseball as the Oakland Athletics affiliate at Jackson Field in downtown Lansing. The ballpark offers affordable family entertainment with great sightlines, fun promotions, and between-inning entertainment. Fireworks nights, theme nights, and special promotions add excitement throughout the season. The ballpark features local food vendors alongside classic ballpark fare. The compact stadium creates an intimate atmosphere where fans feel close to the action. Season runs April through September. The downtown location means easy access to restaurants and bars before or after games. Watching future major league players develop their skills provides exciting baseball at accessible prices. Group outings and birthday packages are popular for families and organizations.

Location: 505 E Michigan Ave, Lansing | Best Time: April-September; Friday fireworks nights

14. Horrocks Farm Market

Horrocks has grown from a small farm stand into a massive market that's become a Lansing institution and regional destination. The expansive store features fresh produce, international foods, extensive cheese and deli selections, prepared foods, and a full grocery. The garden center offers plants, gardening supplies, and expert advice. The floral department creates stunning arrangements. Regular cooking demonstrations and sampling stations make shopping an experience. The market sources from local farms when possible while offering specialty items from around the world. The cafe serves breakfast and lunch. The market's scale and variety amaze first-time visitors. It's much more than a grocery store ‚Äì it's a foodie destination that draws shoppers from across mid-Michigan for specialty ingredients and fresh selections.

Location: 7420 W Saginaw Hwy, Lansing | Best Time: Weekday mornings to avoid weekend crowds

15. Adado Riverfront Park

Adado Riverfront Park sits in downtown Lansing along the Grand River, providing green space for community events and relaxation. The park features an amphitheater hosting free summer concerts, festivals, and special events. The River Trail runs through the park connecting to the larger trail system. Sculptures and public art installations add interest. The park includes playground equipment for children and open lawns for picnicking. The park's proximity to downtown restaurants and attractions makes it a popular gathering spot. Summer brings weekly concert series and events like the Old Town Blues Festival. The park demonstrates urban green space design that brings community together while providing beautiful riverfront access in the heart of the capital city.

Location: 201 E Shiawassee St, Lansing | Best Time: Summer for concerts and events
