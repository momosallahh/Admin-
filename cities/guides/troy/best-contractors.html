üìã Hiring Contractors in Troy: Essential Tips

Before Hiring Any Contractor:

1. Verify Licenses & Insurance
  • Michigan contractor license (builders, electricians, plumbers)
  • General liability insurance ($1M minimum for Troy homes)
  • Workers compensation insurance
  • Call insurance company to verify coverage is current
2. Check References & Reviews
  • Request 5-10 recent Troy references
  • Drive by completed projects to see quality
  • Check Google, Yelp, BBB, Angie's List
  • Look for patterns in feedback (good and bad)
  • Verify photos are actually their work
3. Get Multiple Detailed Quotes
  • Minimum 3 quotes for comparison
  • Ensure quotes include same scope of work
  • Detailed line-item breakdown (not just lump sum)
  • Beware quotes 20%+ lower than others (red flag)
  • Premium contractors cost more but deliver quality
4. Understand Troy-Specific Requirements
  • Building Permits: Required for most major work (contractor should handle)
  • HOA Approval: Necessary for exterior changes in most neighborhoods
  • Setbacks & Codes: City of Troy has specific requirements
  • Timeline: Permit approval usually 1-2 weeks
5. Contract Essentials
  • Written contract for every project (no exceptions)
  • Detailed scope of work and specifications
  • Start and completion dates with penalties
  • Payment schedule (never more than 10% deposit)
  • Change order process clearly defined
  • Warranty terms in writing
  • What happens if problems arise
6. Payment Protection
  • Standard: 10% deposit, progress payments, 10% held until completion
  • Never pay 100% upfront (major red flag)
  • Avoid cash-only contractors (legitimate businesses accept checks/cards)
  • Use credit cards for deposits when possible (fraud protection)
  • Don't make final payment until 100% satisfied
