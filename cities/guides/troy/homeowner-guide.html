Essential Guide for Troy Homeowners

Owning a home in Troy means protecting a significant investment (median $300,000) while navigating city services, HOA requirements, seasonal maintenance, and Michigan's challenging weather. This comprehensive guide covers everything Troy homeowners need to know for successful home ownership.

üè† Your First Year as Troy Homeowner

Essential First-Year Tasks:

Immediately After Purchase:
  • Change Locks: $150-$300 for whole-house rekey
  • Transfer Utilities: DTE Energy (electric/gas), City water
  • Schedule Roof Inspection: Elite Roofing offers free inspections
  • Test All Systems: HVAC, plumbing, electrical
  • Locate Shut-offs: Water main, gas, electrical panel
  • Review HOA Rules: If applicable
First Month:
  • File Principal Residence Exemption (PRE): Reduces property taxes - file with Troy assessor
  • Update Insurance: Ensure adequate coverage ($300K+ homes need sufficient liability)
  • HVAC Service: $150-$200 tune-up ensures efficiency
  • Gutter Cleaning: $100-$200, essential before winter
  • Winterization: If purchasing in fall
First Year:
  • Experience all four seasons in your home
  • Identify any recurring issues (leaks, drafts, etc.)
  • Establish service provider relationships
  • Budget for actual utility costs
  • Plan major improvements or updates

üìÖ Seasonal Maintenance Schedule

Spring (March-May):

  • Roof Inspection: Check for winter damage, missing shingles ($0 with Elite Roofing)
  • Gutter Cleaning: Remove winter debris ($100-$200)
  • HVAC Service: A/C tune-up before summer ($100-$150)
  • Lawn Care Start: Fertilization, weed control ($50-$90/month)
  • Power Wash: Siding, deck, driveway ($200-$400)
  • Window Screens: Clean and repair
  • Irrigation System: Start-up and inspect ($75-$125)

Summer (June-August):

  • Lawn Maintenance: Weekly mowing ($35-$55/week)
  • Tree Trimming: Remove branches near roof ($200-$500)
  • Deck/Fence Staining: Every 2-3 years ($500-$1,500)
  • Window Cleaning: Interior/exterior ($150-$300)
  • Check Roof Ventilation: Ensures attic stays cool
  • Pest Control: Summer treatment ($80-$150)

Fall (September-November):

  • Gutter Cleaning: Critical before winter ($100-$200)
  • Furnace Service: Before heating season ($100-$150)
  • Winterization: Outdoor faucets, irrigation system ($75-$125 winterization)
  • Chimney Cleaning: If you have fireplace ($150-$250)
  • Storm Windows: Install if applicable
  • Lawn Winterization: Final fertilization
  • Snow Equipment: Test snow blower, stock salt/ice melt

Winter (December-February):

  • Snow Removal: Driveway/sidewalk ($35-$60 per visit or $500-$1,200 seasonal contract)
  • Ice Dam Prevention: Keep gutters clear, monitor roof
  • Prevent Frozen Pipes: Insulate exposed pipes, maintain heat
  • Check Attic: Ensure proper insulation and ventilation
  • Monitor for Ice Dams: Remove snow if accumulation excessive
  • Test Backup Systems: Sump pump, generator if applicable

üèóÔ∏è Permits & Building Codes

When You Need a Permit in Troy:

Requires Permit:
  • Roof Replacement: Required ($50-$100 permit)
  • Deck Construction: Required
  • Fence Installation: Required (height restrictions apply)
  • HVAC Replacement: Required
  • Electrical Work: Major work requires permit
  • Plumbing: Major work requires permit
  • Additions: All structural work
  • Basement Finishing: Required
No Permit Needed:
  • Interior painting
  • Carpet/flooring
  • Kitchen/bath updates (non-structural)
  • Landscaping
  • Minor repairs
Permit Process:
  • Apply: Troy Building Department, online or in-person
  • Timeline: 1-2 weeks typical approval
  • Inspections: Required at various stages
  • Contractor Responsibility: Most contractors handle permitting

üèòÔ∏è HOA Living in Troy

Homeowners Association Considerations:

Many Troy neighborhoods (Northfield Hills, Somerset areas) have HOAs governing property standards.

Common HOA Rules:
  • Exterior Changes: Approval required for roof colors, siding, major landscaping
  • Maintenance Standards: Lawn care, exterior upkeep required
  • Parking: No commercial vehicles, boat/RV storage restrictions
  • Fences: Style and height restrictions
  • Holiday Decorations: Timing and type restrictions
HOA Approval for Roofing:
  • Most HOAs have approved color palettes
  • Submit color samples before ordering materials
  • Approval typically takes 1-2 weeks
  • Elite Roofing experienced with all Troy HOA requirements
HOA Benefits:
  • Maintains neighborhood aesthetic and property values
  • Common area maintenance
  • Community events and amenities
  • Enforcement of standards protects your investment
