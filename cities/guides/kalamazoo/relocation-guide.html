3-6 Months Before Move:

  • Research Neighborhoods: Explore Westnedge Hill, Milwood, Winchell, Stuart areas
  • Understand Kalamazoo Promise: Learn eligibility and maximum benefits
  • Job Search: Secure employment at Pfizer, Stryker, Bronson, WMU, or other employers

  • Home Inspection: Get professional inspection including roof (Elite Roofing offers free inspections)
  • School Enrollment: Begin KPS enrollment process if you have children
  • Hire Movers: Get quotes from multiple moving companies
  • Declutter: Sell, donate, or discard items you don't need
  • Notify Current Landlord: Give required notice if renting

1 Month Before Move:

  • Change Address: USPS forwarding, update accounts, notify contacts
  • Transfer Utilities: Set up electric (Consumers Energy), gas, water, internet
  • Register Vehicles: Michigan Secretary of State (14 days after moving)
  • Find Doctor/Dentist: Bronson Healthcare, Borgess Health excellent options
  • Pack Non-Essentials: Start packing items you don't use daily
  • Research Activities: Find parks, gyms, restaurants, entertainment

Move Week:

  • Final Packing: Pack remaining essentials, prepare "open first" box
  • Clean Old Home: Leave in good condition
  • Travel to Kalamazoo: Allow extra time in winter for weather
  • Walk-Through New Home: Check condition, test systems
  • Utilities Check: Ensure all services working
  • Meet Neighbors: Introduce yourself to create connections

First Month:

  • Michigan Driver's License: Visit Secretary of State branch
  • School Start: Begin classes, meet teachers
  • Voter Registration: Register to vote in Michigan
  • Find Favorite Places: Grocery stores, coffee shops, parks
  • Home Maintenance: Schedule roof inspection, furnace check before winter
  • Join Community: Neighborhood associations, clubs, activities

üéì Understanding the Kalamazoo Promise

Critical Information for New Residents:

What Is It?

Anonymous donors created the Kalamazoo Promise in 2005 to provide free college tuition to graduates of Kalamazoo Public Schools. It's one of the nation's most generous scholarship programs and a primary reason families relocate to Kalamazoo.

Eligibility Rules:
  • Residency: Must live in Kalamazoo Public Schools district
  • Enrollment: Continuous KPS enrollment determines percentage covered
  • 4 years (grades 9-12): 65% of tuition covered
  • 7 years (grades 6-12): 80% of tuition covered
  • K-12 enrollment: 100% of tuition and mandatory fees covered
  • Graduation: Must graduate from Kalamazoo Central or Loy Norrix
  • College Choice: Any Michigan public university or community college
Strategic Planning for New Families:
  • Move before kindergarten starts ‚Üí Maximum 100% benefit
  • Enroll in KPS immediately ‚Üí Start accumulating years
  • Stay continuously enrolled ‚Üí Don't reset the clock
  • Understand district boundaries ‚Üí Confirm home location qualifies
Financial Impact Example:
  • University of Michigan annual cost: ~$30,000 (4 years = $120,000)
  • With 100% Promise coverage: $0 tuition cost
  • Family savings: $120,000 per child
  • Two children: $240,000 in total college savings

Bottom Line: The earlier you move and enroll children, the more you save.

üå¶Ô∏è Preparing for Michigan Weather

What Newcomers Need to Know:

Winter Essentials (November-March):
  • Snow: Expect 55+ inches annually (lake-effect snow common)
  • Wardrobe: Heavy winter coat, boots, gloves, hats essential
  • Vehicle: Snow tires recommended, ice scraper required
  • Home: Ensure furnace serviced, roof inspected before winter
  • Driving: Learn winter driving skills, allow extra time
  • Activities: Embrace winter - skiing, sledding, ice skating!
Summer (June-August):
  • Warm, humid (80-90¬∞F typical)
  • Thunderstorms common
  • Air conditioning highly recommended
  • Enjoy outdoor activities, festivals, beaches
Spring/Fall:
  • Beautiful transitional seasons
  • Spring: muddy, unpredictable (March-May)
  • Fall: spectacular colors (September-November)
