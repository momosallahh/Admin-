‚úÖ How to Choose a Warren Contractor

Essential Criteria:

1. Licensing & Insurance (Non-Negotiable)
  • Michigan Residential Builder License (verify at michigan.gov/lara)
  • General Liability Insurance ($1M+ coverage)
  • Workers' Compensation Insurance
  • Warren Business License

Warning: NEVER hire unlicensed contractors - you have zero recourse if issues arise.

2. Local Warren Experience

Choose contractors familiar with:

  • Warren building codes and permit processes
  • Macomb County inspection requirements
  • Michigan climate challenges (cold winters, ice dams, etc.)
  • Warren's typical housing stock (1950s-70s ranches, etc.)
3. References & Reviews
  • Ask for 5+ recent Warren references
  • Check Google, Yelp, BBB ratings
  • Drive by completed projects if possible
  • Talk to past customers about communication, quality, cleanup
4. Detailed Written Estimates

Quality estimates should include:

  • Itemized material costs with brands/models
  • Labor costs broken down by phase
  • Timeline with start/end dates
  • Payment schedule (typically 10-30% deposit, progress payments, 10% final)
  • Warranty terms on labor and materials
  • Permit costs and who handles them
5. Written Contract

MUST include:

  • Scope of work in detail
  • Materials specifications
  • Timeline and milestones
  • Payment schedule tied to milestones
  • Change order process
  • Warranty information
  • Lien waivers

üö© Red Flags - Avoid These Contractors

Warning Signs:

  • No License/Insurance: Walk away immediately
  • Cash-Only: Legitimate businesses accept multiple payment methods
  • Large Upfront Payment: >30% deposit is suspicious
  • No Physical Address: P.O. boxes or no local presence
  • Pressure Tactics: "Today only" pricing is a scam
  • No Written Contract: Verbal agreements are worthless
  • Vague Timeline: Should have specific start/end dates
  • Can't Provide References: Legitimate contractors have satisfied customers
  • Unusually Low Bid: If 40-50% cheaper, they're cutting corners
  • No Permits: Cutting corners that will cost you later

üìã Warren Building Permits Guide

When You Need Permits in Warren:

Always Required:
  • Structural changes (removing walls, additions)
  • Electrical work (panel upgrades, new circuits)
  • Plumbing (new fixtures, water heater, gas lines)
  • HVAC installation/replacement
  • Roof replacement
  • Foundation work
  • Deck construction (>30" high)
  • Fence installation (check height limits)
Generally Not Required:
  • Painting (interior/exterior)
  • Flooring replacement
  • Cabinet installation (no plumbing/electrical changes)
  • Minor repairs
