Warren's Parks & Recreation System

Warren, Michigan's largest suburb with 139,000 residents, maintains an excellent parks system featuring 24+ parks, the award-winning Warren Community Center, sports complexes, and family-friendly recreation spaces. From neighborhood playgrounds to major athletic facilities, Warren provides outstanding outdoor amenities.

The Warren Community Center is one of the finest recreation facilities in Michigan, offering world-class amenities for all ages and abilities.

üèÉ Walking & Biking Trails

Warren Trail Network:

1. Clinton River Trail (nearby)

Length: 16+ miles (sections accessible from Warren)
Surface: Paved

While not entirely in Warren, portions of this excellent trail system are easily accessible for Warren residents, offering scenic river views and connections to regional trail networks.

2. Park Walking Paths

Most Warren parks feature internal walking paths including:

  • Halmich Park: Paved loop trails
  • Warren Community Center: Walking paths on grounds
  • Fitzgerald Park: Perimeter walking/jogging path
3. Neighborhood Sidewalk Networks

Warren features extensive sidewalk systems ideal for walking, especially in:

  • Warren Woods subdivision
  • Near shopping districts (Van Dyke, Mound corridors)
  • Residential neighborhoods near schools

üêï Dog-Friendly Spaces

Dogs in Warren Parks:

Leash Rules:

Dogs are allowed in most Warren parks but must be on leash at all times. Clean up after your pet (waste bags typically not provided - bring your own).

Best Parks for Dog Walking:
  • Halmich Park: Wide paths, plenty of grass
  • Fitzgerald Park: Large open areas
  • Waldenberg Park: Good walking loops
Note:

Warren does not currently have a designated off-leash dog park. Nearby cities (Sterling Heights, Troy) offer dog parks if needed.
