  • ‚ùå Demands Full Payment Upfront: Professional roofers never require 100% payment before starting. Standard is 10-30% deposit, balance on completion.
  • ‚ùå No Physical Address: Legitimate Grand Rapids contractors have verifiable local offices, not just P.O. boxes or out-of-state addresses.
  • ‚ùå Pushy "Today Only" Tactics: High-pressure sales ("discount expires today") is a manipulation technique. Quality contractors don't need these tactics.
  • ‚ùå Won't Provide Insurance Proof: Any hesitation to provide current insurance certificates is a major red flag. Always verify coverage.

üéØ Questions to Ask Grand Rapids Roofers

Before Hiring, Ask These Questions:

  1. "What is your GAF or CertainTeed certification level?"

    Master Elite/Select ShingleMaster are highest tiers. This matters for warranty and quality.

  2. "How do you handle ice dams specific to Grand Rapids' lake-effect snow?"

    Should discuss ice & water shield, ventilation, insulation, and emergency removal services.

  3. "What's your workmanship warranty?"

    Elite contractors offer lifetime. Anything less than 15 years is concerning.

  4. "Do you have experience in my neighborhood?"

    Ask for references from Heritage Hill, East GR, etc. Local experience matters for HOAs and architecture.

  5. "How do you handle insurance claims?"

    Should have dedicated claims specialists, not just "we'll help if needed."

  6. "What's included in your free inspection?"

    Drone inspections, detailed reports, and multiple options show professionalism.

  7. "What's your typical timeline from quote to completion?"

    Helps set realistic expectations. Quality contractors are often booked 2-4 weeks out during peak season.

  8. "Do you offer 24/7 emergency service?"

    Critical for Grand Rapids' severe weather. Verify they actually respond, not just advertise it.

  9. "What wind rating are your shingles?"

    Class F (110 mph) minimum recommended for Grand Rapids due to Lake Michigan winds.

  10. "Can I see your liability insurance certificate?"

    Should provide immediately without hesitation. Verify it's current by calling their insurer.
