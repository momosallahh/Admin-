üíº Employment & Economy

  • General Motors: Still largest employer, Flint Assembly & other facilities
  • McLaren Regional Medical Center: Healthcare jobs
  • Kettering University: Education, research
  • University of Michigan-Flint: Education sector
  • Diplomat Pharmacy: Healthcare/pharmacy

  • Education: University jobs competitive but available
  • Manufacturing: Still present but greatly reduced from peak
  • Retail/Service: Available but lower-paying
  • Median Income: $28,000 individual, $42,000 household (below state average)

Recommendation:

Line up employment BEFORE moving. Job market is competitive. Many Flint residents commute to Detroit/Grand Rapids for work.

üè† Home Buying Process in Flint

Step-by-Step:

1. Research Neighborhoods Thoroughly
  • Visit multiple times (day and night)
  • Check crime stats but don't rely solely on them
  • Talk to current residents
  • Drive 5-block radius around target homes
2. Get Pre-Approved for Financing
  • Challenge: Many lenders won't finance sub-$50K homes
  • Options: Local credit unions, community lenders, or cash purchase
  • FHA loans: Require homes meet strict standards (many Flint homes don't)
3. Work with Local Realtor
  • Choose someone who knows Flint well
  • Ask about specific neighborhood conditions
  • Get honest assessment of home condition
4. CRITICAL: Home Inspection
  • Non-negotiable: ALWAYS get professional inspection
  • Roof inspection essential: Can be $6K-12K expense if needed
  • Check foundation, mechanicals, plumbing, electrical
  • Water testing recommended
  • Cost: $400-600 for inspection (worth every penny)
5. Budget for Repairs
  • Assume $10K-20K minimum for livability repairs
  • Many Flint homes need work even if "move-in ready"
  • Set aside emergency fund
6. Close and Move In
  • Closing costs typically $2K-4K
  • Set up utilities immediately
  • Change locks first day
  • Address urgent repairs (roof, heat) before winter

‚ö†Ô∏è Honest Challenges

What You Should Know:

1. Water Crisis Legacy
  • 2014-2019 crisis damaged city's reputation
  • Water infrastructure largely fixed but stigma remains
  • Many residents still use filters as precaution
  • Test your water independently for peace of mind
2. Crime
  • Higher than state average (though improving)
  • Varies GREATLY by neighborhood
  • College Cultural, Burton, Grand Blanc much safer
  • Research specific blocks, not just city-wide stats
3. Schools
  • Flint Community Schools struggle academically
  • Suburban districts (Grand Blanc, Carman-Ainsworth) much better
  • Charter schools provide alternatives
  • Families with kids usually choose suburban districts
4. Economic Challenges
  • Lower median incomes than state average
  • Job market competitive
  • Manufacturing decline ongoing
  • Many residents commute to other cities for work
5. Harsh Winters
  • 40+ inches snow annually
  • Extremely cold (sub-zero common)
  • Winter driving hazardous
  • High heating costs ($150-300/month winter)
  • Home winterization essential

üè† Protecting Your Flint Home Investment

Critical First Steps After Moving In:

1. Roof Inspection (PRIORITY #1)

Michigan winters destroy bad roofs. Your $40K home investment can be ruined by a $10K roof failure. Get FREE professional inspection immediately.

2. Winterization
  • Ensure furnace works properly
  • Check/improve attic insulation
  • Seal air leaks around windows/doors
  • Prevent pipes from freezing
3. Emergency Fund
  • Keep $3K-5K for unexpected repairs
  • Older homes = maintenance needs
