üìä The Reality Check: What You MUST Know

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Information Before Buying in Flint:

1. Extreme Affordability Has Reasons:
  • Water Crisis: 2014 crisis damaged city's reputation, depressed values
  • Population Decline: Flint lost 50%+ population since peak (156K ‚Üí 95K)
  • Economic Challenges: GM plant closures, job losses
  • Crime: Higher crime rates than state average (though improving)
  • Schools: Flint Community Schools struggle academically
  • City Services: Limited budget affects services quality
2. Not All $30K Homes Are Equal:
  • Some are gems: Need cosmetic updates but structurally sound
  • Many need major work: $20K-40K in repairs to be livable
  • Some are teardowns: Beyond repair, should be demolished
  • LESSON: $30K purchase + $30K repairs = $60K total. Factor ALL costs.
3. Financing Challenges:
  • Many banks won't finance homes under $50K
  • FHA requires homes meet strict standards (many Flint homes don't)
  • Cash purchases common/preferred
  • Hard money or local lenders may be necessary
4. Hidden Costs:
  • Roof: $6,000-12,000 for replacement (very common need)
  • Furnace: $3,000-6,000 (Michigan winters = must have heat)
  • Plumbing: Lead pipe replacement, old galvanized pipes
  • Electrical: Knob-and-tube wiring, outdated panels
  • Foundation: Settlement issues in some areas

‚úì How to Buy Smart in Flint

Strategic Buying Guide:

1. Choose Location Very Carefully:
  • Best bet: College Cultural, Burton, Grand Blanc Township
  • Research thoroughly: Visit multiple times, day and night
  • Check blocks: One good block can border rough area
  • Talk to neighbors: They know reality of living there
2. Inspect EVERYTHING:
  • Professional inspection mandatory: Don't skip to save $400
  • Roof inspection critical: Michigan winters destroy bad roofs
  • Foundation check: Look for cracks, settling, water
  • Mechanicals: Furnace, water heater, electrical, plumbing
  • Water test: Even though crisis "over," test your water
3. Budget for Repairs:
  • Assume minimum $10K-20K for livability repairs
  • Set aside emergency fund ($5K minimum)
  • Factor ongoing maintenance ($200-400/month)
  • Remember: Older homes = constant upkeep
4. Understand Total Cost of Ownership:
Cost Annual Amount
Property Tax $800-1,500
Homeowner's Insurance $600-1,200
Utilities (heat, water, electric) $2,400-3,600
Maintenance/Repairs $2,000-4,000
TOTAL $5,800-10,300/year

Monthly: $480-860 for cash-purchased home (no mortgage!)
