‚ôø Accessibility Considerations

Choosing an Accessible Home:

‚úì Key Features to Look For:
  • Single-Story (Ranch): No stairs, easier mobility
  • Wide Doorways: 36" minimum (wheelchair accessible)
  • First-Floor Bedroom/Bath: Essential for aging in place
  • Step-Free Entry: Or ability to add ramp
  • Lever Door Handles: Easier than knobs
  • Walk-In or Roll-In Shower: Safer than tub
  • Grab Bars: Or ability to install
  • Good Lighting: Bright, well-lit spaces
  • Attached Garage: Protected from weather
Neighborhood Accessibility:
  • Sidewalks: Well-maintained, level
  • Grocery/Pharmacy: Within short drive or delivery available
  • Medical Facilities: Easy access
  • Public Transit: If not driving
  • Snow Removal: Available services or manageable

üè† Aging in Place: Home Modifications

Making Your Home Safe & Accessible:

Essential Modifications:
  • Bathroom Safety: Grab bars, walk-in shower, raised toilet
  • Ramps: Entry/exit accessibility
  • Stair Lift: If multi-story home
  • Wider Doorways: Wheelchair/walker accessible
  • Improved Lighting: Prevent falls
  • Lever Handles: Doors and faucets
  • Non-Slip Flooring: Kitchen, bathrooms
