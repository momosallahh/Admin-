The Complete Detroit Homeowner Guide

Owning a home in Detroit comes with unique challenges and opportunities. From protecting your roof against harsh Michigan winters to maintaining historic charm in older homes, this comprehensive guide covers everything Detroit homeowners need to know for year-round maintenance, seasonal preparations, and long-term property protection.

üè† Detroit-Specific Homeownership Challenges

What Makes Detroit Different:

1. Harsh Winter Climate

Detroit's winters bring freezing temperatures (-10¬∞F to 35¬∞F), heavy snow (42+ inches annually), and freeze-thaw cycles that stress roofs, foundations, and plumbing.

2. Older Housing Stock

Many Detroit homes were built 1900-1950, featuring plaster walls, knob-and-tube wiring, galvanized pipes, and outdated systems requiring special care.

3. Ice Dams & Roof Stress

Poor attic ventilation combined with heavy snow creates ice dams that damage roofs, gutters, and cause interior water damage.

4. Foundation Issues

Freeze-thaw soil movement causes foundation cracks and settling, especially in homes built on clay soil common in Detroit.

5. Lead Paint & Asbestos

Homes built before 1978 likely contain lead paint; pre-1980s homes may have asbestos in insulation, tiles, or siding.

üå∏ SPRING (March - May)

Exterior:
  • Roof Inspection: Check for winter damage, missing shingles, ice dam damage

  • Smoke/CO Detectors: Test and replace batteries

  • Prune trees and shrubs

üèöÔ∏è Historic Home Special Considerations

Many Detroit homes are 80-120 years old, requiring special knowledge and care:

Roofing:

  • Original slate or tile roofs may still be serviceable (100+ year lifespan)
  • Complex roof designs with multiple valleys, hips, turrets
  • Proper flashing critical around chimneys, dormers
  • Some historic districts require approval for roof changes

Electrical:

  • Knob-and-tube wiring (pre-1950) should be replaced
  • 60-100 amp service inadequate for modern needs (upgrade to 200 amp)
  • Add GFCI outlets in bathrooms, kitchen (safety)
  • Insurance may require electrical upgrades

Plumbing:

  • Lead or galvanized pipes should be replaced
  • Water pressure issues common
  • Sewer lines may need camera inspection (tree roots)
  • Consider whole-house water filtration

Insulation:

  • Older homes often under-insulated
  • Blown-in insulation can be added to walls
  • Attic should have R-49+ for Michigan
  • Weather-strip windows and doors

Lead Paint:

  • Assume present in pre-1978 homes
  • Encapsulate or remove using EPA-certified contractors
  • Critical if young children in home
  • Test before renovation projects

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Detroit Home Problems & Solutions

Problem: Ice Dams

Cause: Warm attic melts snow ‚Üí water refreezes at cold eaves

Solutions:

  • Improve attic insulation (R-49+)
  • Increase attic ventilation (soffit + ridge vents)
  • Seal attic air leaks
  • Remove snow from roof edges after heavy storms
  • Install ice and water shield during roof replacement

Cost to Fix: $500-$3,000 depending on solution

Problem: Frozen Pipes

Cause: Exposed pipes in unheated areas during extreme cold

Prevention:

  • Insulate pipes in basement, crawl space, exterior walls
  • Keep cabinet doors open (pipes under sinks)
  • Let faucets drip when below -10¬∞F
  • Maintain minimum 55¬∞F indoor temperature

If Frozen: Call plumber immediately - don't thaw with torch

Problem: Foundation Cracks

Cause: Freeze-thaw soil movement, settling

Action:

  • Small hairline cracks (<1/8"): Monitor, often cosmetic
  • Wider cracks (>1/4"): Get structural engineer assessment
  • Horizontal cracks: URGENT - structural concern
  • Ensure proper drainage away from foundation

Cost: $500-$15,000+ depending on severity

Problem: Basement Water Intrusion

Causes: Poor grading, failed sump pump, foundation cracks, heavy rain/snow melt

Solutions:

  • Grade soil 6" drop in first 10' from foundation
  • Clean gutters, extend downspouts 6'+ from house
  • Install/maintain sump pump with battery backup
  • Interior/exterior waterproofing if persistent
  • Consider French drain system

Cost: $1,000-$10,000+ for waterproofing

üè† Roof Maintenance: Your #1 Priority

Of all home systems, your roof is the most critical. A failing roof can cause:

  • Interior water damage (walls, ceilings, insulation)
  • Mold and health issues
  • Structural damage (rot, compromised framing)
  • Damaged HVAC equipment and ductwork
  • Ruined belongings and expensive repairs
  • Decreased home value and sale complications
