Common Storm Damage in Sterling Heights

üåßÔ∏è Hail Damage

Sterling Heights experiences severe hail primarily June-August. Hail creates:

  • Bruising (circular areas of granule loss on shingles)
  • Fractured fiberglass mat underneath granules
  • Dented metal flashings, vents, and gutters
  • Cracked or broken shingles from large hailstones
  • Damage often not visible from ground level

Insurance Coverage: Almost always covered under standard homeowners policies. Critical to document and file within policy time limits (typically 1-2 years).

üí® Wind Damage

High winds during thunderstorms and severe weather cause:

  • Missing shingles torn off by uplift forces
  • Lifted shingle edges breaking seal strips
  • Blown-off ridge caps exposing roof peak
  • Damaged flashing around penetrations
  • Debris impact from flying objects

Insurance Coverage: Covered for wind speeds exceeding policy thresholds (typically 50-70 mph). Weather service data documents wind speeds during storms.

üå≥ Falling Trees/Branches

Sterling Heights Meadows and Clinton River area have mature trees creating risks:

  • Punctured shingles and decking from branch impact
  • Structural damage to rafters or trusses
  • Crushed gutters and flashing
  • Emergency water intrusion through openings

Insurance Coverage: Typically covered regardless of tree ownership if storm-caused. Document that wind/storm caused failure, not gradual decay.

Sterling Heights' 38" annual snowfall creates:

  • Ice dam water infiltration under shingles
  • Gutters torn off by ice weight
  • Structural stress from excessive snow accumulation
  • Freeze-thaw damage to flashing and seals

Insurance Coverage: Varies by policy. Sudden ice dam damage often covered; gradual deterioration usually not. Documentation critical.

Sterling Heights Storm Patterns & Risks

Understanding Local Weather Threats:

Spring (March-May): Severe thunderstorms increase with warming temps. Hail and high winds common. Rapid temperature fluctuations stress roofing materials recovering from winter.

Summer (June-August): Peak severe weather season. Afternoon/evening thunderstorms can produce baseball-sized hail, 60-80 mph straight-line winds, occasional tornadoes. Highest risk period for Sterling Heights roof damage.

Fall (September-November): Continued storm activity though less frequent. Early season snowfall can catch homeowners off-guard. Tree branches weakened by summer storms fail during autumn winds.

Winter (December-February): Ice storms coating everything in ice, heavy snow loads, and extreme cold. While fewer traditional "storms," winter weather causes different damage types requiring insurance claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long do I have to file a storm damage claim?

A: Most policies require claims within 1-2 years of damage. However, file as soon as possible after storms for best results. Delays make it harder to prove damage was storm-related vs. normal wear.

Q: Will filing a claim raise my insurance rates?

A: Storm damage claims (wind, hail, trees) typically don't raise rates significantly as they're "acts of God" beyond your control. However, multiple claims over short periods may affect future insurability. This is still better than paying $10,000+ out-of-pocket for repairs.

Q: What if insurance company denies my claim?

A: We help appeal denials with additional documentation and expert assessments. Many initial denials are overturned on appeal. As a last resort, public adjusters or attorneys may be necessary for complex disputes.

Q: Can you work with any insurance company?

A: Yes. We've worked with all major insurance companies serving Sterling Heights including State Farm, Allstate, Nationwide, Auto-Owners, Farm Bureau, USAA, and many others. Insurance company doesn't matter - process is similar for all.
