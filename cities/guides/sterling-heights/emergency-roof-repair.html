Preventing Future Roofing Emergencies

Proactive Steps Sterling Heights Homeowners Should Take:

  • Annual Inspections: Schedule professional inspection each fall before winter. Identifies problems while they're still minor and addressable during good weather.
  • Tree Maintenance: Trim branches within 10 feet of roof. Especially critical near Dodge Park and Clinton River Trail where mature trees pose risks during storms.
  • Gutter Cleaning: Clean gutters twice yearly (spring/fall). Clogged gutters cause ice dams and water backup under shingles - common Sterling Heights problem.
  • Ventilation Upgrades: Ensure proper attic ventilation to prevent ice dams. If you experience ice dams, upgrade ventilation before next winter - emergency ice dam removal is expensive.
  • Address Minor Issues: Don't ignore small leaks or missing shingles. A $400 repair today prevents $4,000 emergency tomorrow when storm hits.
  • Know Your Roof Age: If roof approaching 20+ years, plan replacement before emergency failure. Proactive replacement costs less than emergency work plus interior damage.
  • Insurance Review: Ensure adequate coverage including emergency services, replacement cost (not actual cash value), and reasonable deductibles.
