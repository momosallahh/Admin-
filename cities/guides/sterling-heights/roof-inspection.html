Common Problems Found in Sterling Heights Roofs

Ice Dam Damage (Most Common Winter Issue)

Occurs when inadequate attic ventilation/insulation allows heat to melt snow, which refreezes at eaves. Water backs up under shingles causing leaks. We find this in 40% of Sterling Heights Meadows homes built before 1990. Solutions include ventilation upgrades and enhanced ice & water shield during replacement.

Inadequate Ventilation

Many older Sterling Heights homes lack proper ridge/soffit ventilation. Results in premature shingle failure, ice dams, and excessive cooling costs. Simple upgrade dramatically extends roof life and improves comfort.

Storm Damage (Hail/Wind)

Sterling Heights sees severe thunderstorms especially summer months. Hail creates bruising (granule loss circles) and wind lifts shingle edges. Often not visible from ground but shows clearly in drone footage. Many homeowners have insurance-covered damage they don't know about.

Failed Flashing

Flashing around chimneys, vents, and penetrations deteriorates from freeze-thaw cycles. Common failure point on 15+ year old roofs. Causes localized leaks that travel far from entry point before appearing inside.

Shingle Deterioration

Sterling Heights' temperature extremes (-10¬∞F to 90¬∞F) stress shingles causing curling, cracking, and granule loss. Accelerates after 20 years. Thermal imaging shows which areas deteriorating fastest.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does a roof inspection take in Sterling Heights?

A: Most inspections take 45-90 minutes. Drone imaging takes 15-20 minutes, attic inspection 15-30 minutes, and ground-level assessment 15-20 minutes. You'll receive preliminary findings on-site with full written report within 24-48 hours.

Q: Do I need to be home during the inspection?

A: Yes, for comprehensive inspections requiring attic access. For exterior-only drone inspections, homeowner presence isn't required. We'll coordinate scheduling that works with your Sterling Heights work schedule including automotive shift workers.

Q: How often should Sterling Heights homes have roof inspections?

A: Annual inspections recommended, ideally fall before winter. Additional inspections after severe storms, before buying/selling homes, or if you notice problems (leaks, missing shingles, ice dams). Homes 15+ years old benefit from more frequent monitoring.

Q: Will inspection damage my roof?

A: No. Our drone inspections are completely non-contact. When we do walk roofs, our experienced inspectors know proper foot placement and techniques that prevent shingle damage. We're fully insured if any issues occur.

Q: Can you inspect roofs in winter?

A: Yes. Drone inspections work year-round. Snow/ice may limit visibility, but we can assess most conditions. Thermal imaging actually works better in winter showing heat loss and ice dam formation areas. We avoid walking on frozen/snow-covered roofs for safety.
