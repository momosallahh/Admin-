Frequently Asked Questions - Sterling Heights Roofing

Q: How long does a roof last in Sterling Heights?

A: With proper installation and maintenance, architectural shingles typically last 25-30 years in Sterling Heights' climate. Premium shingles can last 30-50 years. Metal roofing can last 40-70 years. Our harsh winters with 38" of snow and freeze-thaw cycles make quality installation critical for longevity.

Q: When is the best time to replace a roof in Sterling Heights?

A: Late spring through fall (May-October) offers the best conditions for roofing work. Summer provides optimal shingle sealing temperatures. However, we work year-round and can complete quality installations even during mild winter periods when necessary. Book early for peak season (June-September).

Q: Will my homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

A: Insurance typically covers storm damage (wind, hail), falling trees, and sudden damage. Normal wear and tear or age-related deterioration usually isn't covered. Our insurance specialists can inspect your roof, document storm damage, and work directly with your adjuster to maximize your claim.

Q: Do I need a permit to replace my roof in Sterling Heights?

A: Yes, Sterling Heights requires building permits for roof replacements. We handle all permit applications, inspections, and coordinate with the city's building department. Permit costs are typically $150-300 and included in our estimates.

Q: How much does a typical roof replacement cost in Sterling Heights?

A: For Sterling Heights' typical home sizes, expect $7,500-$10,500 for a 1,500 sq ft ranch, $9,500-$13,500 for a 2,000 sq ft bi-level, and $11,500-$15,000 for a 2,500 sq ft colonial. Costs vary based on materials, complexity, and current roof condition. We offer free estimates and competitive financing options.

Q: What can I do about ice dams on my Sterling Heights home?

A: Ice dams form when heat escapes through your attic, melting snow that refreezes at the eaves. Solutions include: improving attic insulation, installing proper ventilation, adding ice and water shield underlayment, and installing heat cables. We offer comprehensive ice dam prevention during roof replacement.
