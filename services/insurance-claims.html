Insurance Claims FAQs

Common questions about roof insurance claims

Do I have to pay anything upfront for claim assistance?

No! Our claim assistance is completely free. We only get paid when your insurance pays us for the approved work. You'll only pay your insurance deductible, which goes directly to the insurance company. We're motivated to maximize your claim because our payment depends on claim approval.

Should I contact my insurance company before or after getting an inspection?

Get our free inspection first! We'll assess whether you have legitimate claim-worthy damage before you file. Filing claims without damage can lead to policy cancellation. Once we confirm damage, we'll help you file and prepare all documentation before the adjuster arrives.

What if the insurance adjuster and roofer disagree on damage?

This is common and why our presence at inspections is valuable. We professionally document disagreements, provide additional evidence, and request supervisory review if needed. We use the same estimating software as adjusters (Xactimate) so our estimates are credible and detailed.

How long does a typical roof insurance claim take?

Most claims are resolved within 2-4 weeks. The timeline includes filing (1-3 days), adjuster inspection (3-10 days), estimate review (3-7 days), and approval (1-3 days). We expedite where possible and keep you informed throughout. Complex claims or supplements may take longer.

Will filing a claim raise my insurance rates?

Storm damage claims (wind, hail, etc.) typically don't raise rates as they're considered "acts of God." However, policies and companies vary. We recommend discussing with your agent. Filing fraudulent or questionable claims can impact rates, which is why we only recommend filing when legitimate damage exists.

What if my claim is denied?

We don't give up! We review the denial reason, gather additional evidence, request re-inspection, and escalate to management if warranted. Many denials are overturned with proper documentation. If the denial is valid, we'll explain why and discuss self-pay repair options at fair pricing.
