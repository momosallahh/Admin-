Metal Roofing FAQs

Common questions about metal roofing systems

How long does a metal roof actually last?

Metal roofs typically last 50-70+ years with minimal maintenance. Many metal roofs from the 1800s are still functional today. Compare this to asphalt shingles that last 15-25 years. A metal roof may be the last roof your home ever needs, making it an excellent long-term investment.

Is metal roofing noisy in the rain?

No! This is a common myth. When properly installed over solid decking with quality underlayment and attic insulation, metal roofs are no noisier than asphalt shingles. The insulation and decking absorb sound effectively. Most homeowners report no noticeable difference in sound levels.

How much does metal roofing cost compared to shingles?

Metal roofing typically costs 2-3x more upfront than asphalt shingles. However, when you factor in the 50+ year lifespan (vs 20 years for shingles), energy savings, insurance discounts, and increased home value, metal roofing often costs less over the life of your home. Expect $15,000-$25,000 for most residential metal roofs.

Can metal roofing be installed over existing shingles?

While technically possible, we recommend complete tear-off for metal roofing. This allows us to inspect and repair the deck, install proper underlayment, and ensure optimal performance. Installing over shingles can trap moisture, hide damage, and void warranties. A clean deck is the foundation of a long-lasting metal roof.

Will a metal roof increase my home's value?

Yes! Metal roofing typically provides 85-95% ROI at resale. Buyers appreciate the durability, energy efficiency, and knowing they won't need to replace the roof for decades. Metal roofs are particularly attractive in areas prone to severe weather, and many insurance companies offer premium discounts for metal roofing.

Does metal roofing attract lightning?

No, metal roofing does NOT attract lightning. Lightning strikes the highest point in an area regardless of material. However, if lightning does strike a metal roof, the metal safely disperses the electrical charge and is non-combustible, making it safer than other roofing materials. Metal roofs receive the highest fire rating (Class A).
